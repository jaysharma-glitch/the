HELLO!

I'm Suchitra Ayappa, a fourth generation coffee planter, and I hail from the hills of Coorg in Karnataka, India's Primary coffee producing region.

Before I tell you more, I want to inform you that all images of the estate are raw unfiltered images taken by me so you can see it all in its true form.

My Dad and I at our Estate


Funny thing about inheritance.. just when you think the only thing your grandparents gave you was their brown skin, arthritis and a stack of old Harold Robbins novels, you realise you are now the keeper of acres of coffee plantation amidst the mountains in Coorg.

Our Family Home


At first one knows not how to react on inheriting the very land where one spent carefree childhood summers climbing haystacks and teasing farm animals.. but once it sinks in, the joy is insurmountable.

Coffee Plantations at our estate in Alankar


Our coffee is grown on our plantation on an estate called Alankar. The mature coffee plants are shaded amidst thickly forested land, alongside sandal wood ,rose wood , silver oak and teak and cocoa trees on gentle slopes

The Coffee Cherry


We grow both Arabica as well as Robusta. The plants are mature and sturdy and most importantly, free of chemical pest deterrents. We are certified by the Rainforest Alliance known globally to keep all things clean & organic.

The Yellow Bohemian Coffee Hamper


I spent a few summers creating my own blends and roasts in my kitchen on the estates, and have come up with a blend that I personally favour, using only grade Triple A Arabica beans, machine roasted to perfection to a dark roast.

Me with the Probat Roasters at our estate


The whole beans are roasted to perfection in Probat roasters - The finest in German Engineering because the secret to a great cup of coffee lies in proper roasting.
